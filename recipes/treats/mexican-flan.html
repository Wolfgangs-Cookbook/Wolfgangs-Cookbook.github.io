Mexican Flan

Silky Smooth Caramel Custard

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar (for caramel)
  • 1 14oz can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 12oz can evaporated milk
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375Â°F. Have ready a 9-inch cake pan and a larger baking dish for the water bath.
  2. Make the caramel: Heat sugar in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly until it melts and turns golden amber (about 8-10 minutes). Add vanilla and stir, then immediately pour into cake pan, swirling to coat bottom.
  3. In a large bowl or blender, mix/whisk together condensed milk, evaporated milk, and eggs until smooth. Avoid creating bubbles.
  4. Strain mixture through a fine-mesh sieve (preferred), or pour into the caramel-lined pan.
  5. Place cake pan in larger baking dish. Fill outer dish with hot water most of the way up the sides of the flan pan.
  6. Bake for 45-55 minutes until center is just set but still jiggly (like gelatin), and a toothbick comes out barely dry (or slightly wet if preferred).
  7. Remove from water bath and cool completely.
  8. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  9. To serve, run a knife around the edge and invert onto a rimmed serving plate.

Tips

  • Watch the caramel closely as it can burn quickly
  • Straining ensures silky smooth texture
  • Don't overbake - flan should be barely set when done
  • Can be made up to 3 days ahead
