Mapo Tofu

A bold, spicy Sichuan classic—make the sauce once, use it on anything

Mapo Sauce Base

  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp doubanjiang (or substitute 1 tbsp miso + 1 tbsp chili crisp)
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns (or 1/2 tsp black pepper + 1/2 tsp lemon zest for a tingling vibe)
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chicken or mushroom broth
  • Optional umami boost: 2 tbsp shiitake broth or 1 tsp black garlic paste*
  • 1 tsp cornstarch + 1 tbsp water (for thickening)

*Black garlic is garlic slowly fermented until sweet, earthy, and jammy. If unavailable, mash a roasted garlic clove with a drop of balsamic or molasses. Shiitake broth can be made by soaking dried mushrooms in warm water for 30+ minutes.

To Make Mapo Tofu

  • 1 block soft or medium tofu, cut into 3/4" cubes
  • Optional: 1/4 lb ground pork, ground turkey, or ground mushrooms
  • 1 scallion, sliced, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium. Sauté garlic and ginger for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add doubanjiang (or sub) and stir-fry for 1–2 minutes until oil turns red and aromatic.
  3. Stir in soy sauce, ground Sichuan peppercorn, and sugar if using.
  4. Add broth (and optional umami boosters) and bring to a simmer.
  5. Stir in cornstarch slurry and simmer until slightly thickened. This is your Mapo sauce—use now or refrigerate up to 5 days.
  6. To make tofu version: Gently add tofu to the simmering sauce. Simmer 5–6 minutes to absorb flavor.
  7. Garnish with scallions and serve over steamed rice.

Tips

  • Use firm tofu for cleaner presentation, or soft tofu for a more comforting texture.
  • Try the sauce on noodles, roasted veggies, or spooned over rice with a fried egg.
  • If sensitive to spice, reduce doubanjiang or chili and balance with more garlic and soy.
