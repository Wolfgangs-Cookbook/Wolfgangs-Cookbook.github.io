Low-Salt Biga Neapolitan Dough

Classic 65% Hydration with an Overnight Biga Preferment

Note that this recipe is unfinished and needs to be updated

Ingredients (3 pizzas)

  • 500g 00 flour (50% Caputo Pizzeria, 50% Caputo Chef's)
  • 325g water (65% hydration total)
  • 0.3g active dry yeast (for biga)
  • 4g salt

Instructions

  1. Make the Biga (the day before): Mix 330g flour, 165g water (room temp), and 0.3g yeast until just combined. Leave in a covered bowl or tub at 18–20°C (64–68°F) for 12–16 hours.
  2. Final Dough: Add the remaining 170g flour, 160g water, and 4g salt to the biga. Mix until combined and knead until smooth (hand or machine).
  3. Rest covered at room temperature for 1 hour, then perform 2–3 stretch and folds, 20 minutes apart.
  4. Transfer to the fridge for a cold bulk ferment for 24–72 hours.
  5. Remove from fridge 4–6 hours before baking. Divide into 3 dough balls (~275g), shape, and rest at room temperature until airy and pliable.
  6. Stretch gently into 10–11 inch rounds. Top and bake on a steel or stone at 500–550°F for 6–8 minutes or until puffy and charred.

Tips

  • The biga should look dry and webby when ready — resist adding more water at first.
  • Try 48–72 hours total ferment for better flavor and digestibility.
  • Salt is reduced in this dough to suit low-sodium diets; adjust sauce and toppings accordingly.
