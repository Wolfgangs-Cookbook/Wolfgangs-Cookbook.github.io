Pizza Recipes

🧠 Overview

Pizza doughs vary widely, but they all begin with the same ingredients: flour, water, yeast, and salt. In this cookbook, we focus on low-sodium doughs, which need careful handling to maintain flavor and structure. Low salt often means using less yeast, allowing for longer fermentations that build complexity and digestibility.

We’ll explore:

  • Bulk Fermentation: A single-stage, longer fermentation that requires less handling and develops great depth over time.
  • Biga: A firm Italian preferment that gives lightness and complexity.
  • Poolish: A wetter, more active pre-ferment that enhances extensibility and browning.
  • Flour choices: We favor high-protein bread flours or tipo 00 for strength and chew.
