Poolish Pizza Dough

Light, bubbly dough made with an overnight preferment for deep flavor and great chew

Note that this recipe is just a template and will be updated once finalized.

Ingredients

For the Poolish (Make the Night Before)

  • 250g all-purpose or bread flour
  • 250g water (room temp)
  • 0.2g active dry yeast (a pinch — about 1/16 tsp)

For the Final Dough

  • 250g all-purpose or bread flour
  • 75g water (cold)
  • 4g salt (about 1 tsp)
  • 0.2g active dry yeast (another pinch)
  • All of the poolish

Instructions

  1. Make the poolish: Stir together the flour, water, and yeast in a bowl. Cover loosely and let sit at room temperature for 12–16 hours until bubbly and doubled.
  2. Mix the dough: In a large bowl, add the rest of the flour, salt, yeast, and water. Add the poolish. Mix until shaggy, then knead by hand or mix in a stand mixer until smooth and elastic (5–8 min).
  3. Bulk ferment: Cover and rest for 1–2 hours at room temperature, doing 1–2 stretch and folds every 30 minutes to build strength.
  4. Divide and shape: Divide into 2 or 3 equal dough balls (depending on your pizza size). Shape gently and place in oiled containers or a floured tray.
  5. Cold proof: Refrigerate for 8–48 hours for best flavor. Bring to room temperature 1–2 hours before baking.
  6. Stretch and bake: Stretch by hand, top as desired, and bake on a preheated stone or steel at 500°F+ until golden and bubbly (6–9 minutes).

Tips

  • Hydration is 65%, great for home oven use—easy to stretch but still bubbly.
  • If short on time, increase yeast to 1g in the final dough and shorten fridge proof to 4–6 hours.
  • Use bread flour for more chew, or a mix of AP + bread flour for balance.
